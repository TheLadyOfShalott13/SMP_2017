Academics and Student Life at VNIT

There are eight B.Tech departments at VNIT and one B.Arch programme at the undergraduate level. Each department has their own website, syllabus and a dedicated team of specialized professors who teach every subject that is included in the curriculum for the student enrolled. Following is a list of departments for undergraduate degrees in both the Engineering and Architecture Disciplines.
Architecture and Planning
Chemical Engineering
Civil Engineering
Computer Science and Engineering
Electronics and Communication Engineering
Electrical and Electronics Communication
Mechanical Engineering
Metallurgy Engineering
Mining Engineering
Each B.Tech programme consists of 8 semesters and the B.Arch programme consists of 10 semesters. At the end of the course, students must complete atleast 320 credits. In each semester, students must register themselves for DC (Compulsory courses), DE (Elective courses), OC (Optional courses), HM (Humanity Courses) and AU (Audit Courses) - cumulative credit points and cgpa scores are calculated per semester. This also includes practical courses(labs), workshops and the final year thesis.

Grading per course is decided by the Professor incharge of that course. Any student that fails to cross the minimum pass score at the end of each course per semester will have to retake the final exam as a re-exam during the holidays. If a student fails to pass at this attempt, the subject becomes a backlog and must be registered again in the consecutive odd or even semester. For first year students, there is a special provision for clearing all the backlogs in the first year - Summer Term - a 2-3 month course that takes place during the summer break of the academic year. No student is allowed to have more than 8 backlog subjects at a time. If any student is found to be consistent in poor academic performance, he/she may be granted a "NFTE" (Not Fit for Technical Education) certificate and may be discontinued from their course.

Each student is assigned a mentor, department faculty advisor and department mentor faculty advisor. Depending on the poor performance of a student, the concerned faculty along with the mentor will work together with the student to improve the student's grades. Students with excellent academic records will be awarded for their performance during the course of their respective programmes.

At VNIT, we strive for excellence and our programmes are tailored to provide enough oppurtunities and resources to all students from all kinds of backgrounds. For more information, please visit the VNIT official website.
