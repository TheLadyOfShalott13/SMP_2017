About the Student Mentorship Programme

What are the objectives of the SMP?
The objectives of the SMP include:
• To help undergraduate fresh students understand the challenges and opportunities present in the Institute and develop a smooth transition to campus life.
• To counsel academically weak undergraduate first year students and to play an important role in helping troubled students cope with academic, extra-academic and personal issues,
• To provide positive role models to first year undergraduate students in the institute.
• To proactively try to identify various issues of concern to the general student populace and to bring them to the notice of the concerned authorities.
• To provide them one such friend among the seniors, with whom they can freely share their problems, ask any queries which they otherwise feel shy to ask or share.

The SMP endeavours to pursue these objectives by carefully identifying those who can act as an anchor and guide for a freshman or an academically weak student to bank upon. Student Mentors are selected to play this role and are given the authority to pursue the mission of this programme.

What kind of students are appointed as student mentors?
Student Mentors are respected, well-balanced students usually belonging to the 3rd year B. Tech and 4th year B.Arch. A student mentor's role may be perceived to be facilitative, supportive and developmental for the student community in general. They must be all-rounders in academic and extra-curricular activities in order to promote a healthy lifestyle at college for the students. They must also have a great EQ (Emotional Intelligence Quota) in order to be empathically able to communicate and relate to their mentees problems.

How is the SMP structured?
This programme primarily deals with first year undergraduate students. The senior students, called Student Mentors (SM) are responsible for helping a set of freshmen adjust to the new environment and subsequently monitor their progress throughout the year. Every undergraduate freshman is covered by this programme. People involved in the Student Mentor Programme:
1) Dean (Academic)
2) Dean (Students Welfare)
3) Professor-in-charge, SMP
4) Associate Dean (Hostel)
5) Student Counsellor
6) Medical Officer
7) Faculty Coordinators - One from each Discipline/Branch
8) Faculty Mentors from Basic Science Department
9) Alumni representation
10) Parent Representation: One Lady and One Gent Student Mentors (SMs)

Selected students from the 3rd year B.Tech and 4th year B.Arch constitute the team of SMs. Each of them would be assigned a group of first year students. Each Student Mentor reports directly to the Faculty co-coordinator. He/she would be the direct point of interaction with the first year students and may report special cases for the consideration of Faculty Coordinator.
